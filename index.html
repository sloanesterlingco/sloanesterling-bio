What MyHealthVaultAI Does

Core functions

MyHealthVaultAI is a standalone health app patients can download themselves. It returns information back to the person living the life — while aligning with the EMR environments most providers already use.

01 · Health vault
All your history in one place

Store medications, allergies, surgeries, medical history, hospitalizations, and visit summaries in a format that can be shared cleanly and consistently.

02 · Safety intelligence
Medication checks & alerts

Track active medications and surface potential contraindications or points of concern before they become issues to discuss with your provider.

03 · Real-time data
Bluetooth health monitoring

Connect compatible Bluetooth devices for vitals and trends that travel with you into each visit, without extra friction for the clinic.

04 · Document capture
OCR for labs & imaging

Use your camera to scan lab results, imaging reports, and office visit letters. MyHealthVaultAI uses OCR to read and organize them inside your vault.

05 · Visit workflow
Structured visit prep

Generate a clean visit summary for check-in, reduce repeated questions at the front desk, and streamline the flow from reception to checkout.

06 · EMR awareness
Built for integration

The system is being shaped to align with the EMR platforms most providers already use, so better experiences can live inside existing workflows — not outside them.

Founder Story

Why this exists

I’m Sloane Sterling — a practicing PA-C and systems-minded builder focused on how health, technology, and real life intersect. MyHealthVaultAI came from watching how much of a patient’s story gets lost between what they remember, what providers have time to ask, and what actually lands in the chart.

The goal isn’t to replace the EMR. It’s to create a patient-first layer that keeps their health details organized, compatible with modern systems, and ready to step into the visit with them — so clinicians can spend more time practicing medicine and less time reconstructing history.

Future Roadmap

In motion

The app is approximately 85–90% built. The current focus is on safe rollout and smart integration:

• Refining medication-safety checks and contraindication logic.
• Expanding Bluetooth support for common home devices (blood pressure, pulse ox, wearables).
• Designing EMR-aligned export formats for clinics that opt in.
• Building patient onboarding flows and education around “owning your story” without overwhelming your care team.
