What MyHealthVaultAI does

Core functions

MyHealthVaultAI is a standalone health app patients can download themselves. It’s built to return health information back to the person living the life — while respecting how busy clinics and providers actually operate behind the scenes.

The system is being shaped to integrate with the electronic medical record platforms that most providers already use, helping reduce friction at check-in, during the visit, and at checkout.

01 · Health vault
All your history in one place
Store medications, allergies, medical history, surgical history, social history, hospitalizations, and office visits — in a format that can be shared cleanly and consistently.
02 · Safety intelligence
Medication checks & alerts
Track your active medications and surface possible contraindications or points of concern before they become issues to discuss with your provider.
03 · Real-time data
Bluetooth health monitoring
Connect compatible Bluetooth devices for real-time vitals and tracking. Bring richer context into your appointments without extra effort.
04 · Document capture
OCR for labs & imaging
Use your camera to scan lab results, imaging reports, and office visit summaries. MyHealthVaultAI uses OCR to read and organize them inside your vault.
05 · Visit workflow
Structured visit prep
Generate a clean visit summary for check-in, reduce repeated questions at the front desk, and streamline the flow from reception to checkout.
06 · EMR awareness
Built for integration
The system is being designed to align with the EMR environments most providers already use, so better experiences can live inside existing workflows—not outside them.

Founder story

Why this exists

I’m Sloane Sterling — a systems-minded builder focused on how health, technology, and real life intersect. MyHealthVaultAI was born from seeing how much of healthcare gets lost in translation: between what patients remember, what providers have time to ask, and what actually ends up inside the chart.

This app is my answer to that gap. It’s designed to give patients control over their own health story while respecting the realities of a busy clinic — a way to help people organize their medical lives and help providers walk into the room with better information from the start.

The goal isn’t to replace care. It’s to clear the path so real care can happen more efficiently, with less friction and more clarity on both sides of the exam room door.
